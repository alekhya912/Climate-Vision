Key Insights

Temperature Trends

Global temperatures have risen by approximately 0.8°C since 1980, with the rate of warming accelerating in recent decades.

CO₂ Concentration

Atmospheric CO₂ has increased from ~338 ppm in 1980 to over 418 ppm in 2023, exceeding pre-industrial levels by more than 45%.

Correlation Patterns

Strong correlation exists between rising CO₂ levels and global temperature increases, with a slight lag in temperature response to atmospheric changes.

Rate of Change

The rate of climate change has accelerated significantly since 2000, with the period 2010-2023 showing the steepest trends across multiple indicators.

Future Climate Scenarios

Explore potential climate futures based on different emission pathways

Low Emissions Scenario

Temperature Change by 2100
+1.5°C
Key Assumptions
Rapid transition to renewables, carbon neutrality by 2050

This scenario assumes ambitious climate policies worldwide, with rapid transition to renewable energy sources, widespread adoption of carbon capture technologies, and significant improvements in energy efficiency. Global net-zero emissions would be achieved by 2050, limiting warming to approximately 1.5°C above pre-industrial levels by 2100.

Projected Impacts

  • Moderate sea level rise (30-60 cm by 2100)
  • Increased frequency of heat waves, but limited to manageable levels
  • Some ecosystem changes, but many natural systems able to adapt
  • Reduced but still significant impacts on agriculture in vulnerable regions

Medium Emissions Scenario

Temperature Change by 2100
+2.7°C
Key Assumptions
Gradual emission reductions, mixed energy sources

This scenario reflects moderate climate policies with a gradual transition away from fossil fuels, but continued reliance on gas and some coal. It assumes uneven implementation of climate policies globally, with developed nations reducing emissions faster than developing countries. Global emissions peak around 2040 and decline thereafter, but do not reach net-zero by 2100.

Projected Impacts

  • Significant sea level rise (50-80 cm by 2100)
  • Substantial increase in heat waves and extreme weather events
  • Major stress on ecosystems, with significant biodiversity loss
  • Considerable agricultural disruption in many regions
  • Increased water stress affecting billions of people

High Emissions Scenario

Temperature Change by 2100
+4.4°C
Key Assumptions
Business-as-usual, continued fossil fuel dependence

This scenario represents a business-as-usual pathway with minimal climate policy intervention. It assumes continued heavy reliance on fossil fuels, high economic growth powered primarily by coal, oil, and gas, and limited deployment of clean energy technologies. Emissions continue to rise throughout most of the century.

Projected Impacts

  • Extreme sea level rise (80-130 cm by 2100, with continued rise thereafter)
  • Deadly heat waves becoming common in many regions
  • Severe and widespread ecosystem collapse
  • Major agricultural failures in multiple breadbasket regions
  • Large-scale human migration due to uninhabitable conditions
  • Multiple tipping points in the climate system potentially crossed
